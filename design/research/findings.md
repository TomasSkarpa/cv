## Research findings

- **Goal**: Inform the Structured Technical Dossier redesign for a working CV with two entry audiences.

### Audience goals

- **Hiring manager (60-second scan)**: Confirm role fit, commerce domain experience, tenure at Bata, and a credible contact path. Needs a clear CV → Projects → Contact path without digging through technical depth.
- **Technical reviewer (depth path)**: Validate stack breadth, project contributions, and delivery approach. Needs Stack → Projects → Professional approach with scannable evidence, not prose walls.

### Competitive positioning

- Avoid the **warm serif CV template** cliché (Literata + cream paper reads generic).
- Avoid the **dark dev portfolio** cliché (signals hobbyist, not enterprise Tech Lead).
- Target a **cool ink dossier**: credible, structured, distinctive type pairing, tiered information density.

### Key scan targets

| Target | Why it matters |
|--------|----------------|
| Role and title | Immediate fit signal for hiring |
| Commerce / SFCC domain | Differentiator from generic full-stack CVs |
| Bata Group tenure | Enterprise scale credibility |
| Flagged It side project | Builder mindset, maintained product |
| Stack depth levels | Technical reviewer confidence |

### Layout decision

- **Top sticky nav** replaces persistent sidebar: frees horizontal space, differentiates home as a landing page.
- **Hybrid shell**: rich home hero + focused prose column on inner pages.
- **PageTOC** on long pages (`/cv`, `/projects`) for section jumping without adding nav depth.

### Design implications

- Home gets hero band, stat strip, pathway cards, and explore grid (not uniform document sections).
- CV gets visual timeline with company anchors and nested role cards.
- Stack/Skills get visual depth meters (text + bar, not color-only).
- All routes, labels, and copy sources stay unchanged.
